Calories and Daily needs

Calories in Vs Calories out is the most important aspect of gaining weight, maintaining weight or losing weight. People should usually eat around 14 calories per bodyweight(to maintain weight). If people want to lose weight they should eat 12 calories per each pound of body weight. To gain weight the individual should eat above their maintance needs. So that should be brought around 16 calories per pound of body weight. A pound of weight is gained or lossed when eating +/- 3500 calories. To set an example, if a man needs 2000 calories to maintain his body weight, he would need to eat 1500 calories 7 days a week since 500 calorie deficit times the 7 days a week is equal to 3500. To gain weight the man would have to eat 2500 calories 7days a week. The 500 excess calories times 7 days a week would be an extra 3500 calories the individual needs through out the day. Hence weight gain to occur.

This happens because many good carbohydrates, especially whole grains and vegetables with skin, contain fiber.
